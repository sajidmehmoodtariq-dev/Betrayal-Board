# 🎲 Betrayal-Board – The Game of Strategy, Suspicion & Survival

Betrayal-Board is a **psychological multiplayer board game** inspired by the classic Ludo board — but redesigned for players who crave strategy, tension, and mind games.

This is not simply a board game about moving pieces across squares.

It is a game about **trust, deception, and timing**.

Players must decide whether to:

* follow the rules honestly
* manipulate the system
* challenge suspicious moves
* sabotage opponents at the perfect moment

Every match becomes a battlefield of **strategy, observation, and calculated betrayal**.

---

## 🧠 Designed for Strategic Minds

Betrayal-Board was created with a simple idea:

> *What if a classic board game could become a psychological battle instead of a game of luck?*

Traditional board games rely heavily on dice rolls.

Betrayal-Board introduces new layers:

✨ **Strategic decision-making**

🎭 **Bluffing and deception**

⚠️ **Risk and consequence mechanics**

🔥 **Chaotic late-game tension**

The result is a game where **no victory is ever guaranteed** until the final moment.

---

## 👥 Multiplayer Experience

Betrayal-Board is designed for **social competition**.

Each match includes:

🎮 **4 – 6 players**

Friends compete on the same board while constantly observing each other's moves.

Every turn creates new questions:

* Was that move legal?
* Did someone just cheat?
* Should I challenge them?
* Should I wait for a better opportunity?

The tension grows as players begin to suspect each other.

---

## 🎯 The Core Objective

Each player controls multiple pieces that must travel around the board and reach their home zone.

The first player to successfully bring **all of their pieces home** wins the match.

However, the journey is far from simple.

Every move exposes players to:

* captures
* penalties
* challenges
* sabotage from opponents

Victory requires both **smart movement and psychological awareness**.

---

## 🎲 Dice & Movement

Movement across the board is determined by dice rolls.

Depending on the number of players, the game may use:

| Players | Dice   |
| ------- | ------ |
| 4       | 1 die  |
| 5–6     | 2 dice |

Players have flexibility in how they use their dice.

They may:

* combine dice values to move a single piece
* move multiple pieces separately

This creates tactical movement choices each turn.

---

## ⚔️ Capturing Opponents

If a player's piece lands on a square occupied by an opponent, the opponent's piece is **captured**.

Captured pieces are sent back to the starting area and must begin their journey again.

Capturing creates moments of excitement — but in Betrayal-Board, even victory has consequences.

---

## 💥 The Near-Finish Catastrophe

One of the most dramatic mechanics occurs near the end of the board.

If a piece is **one step away from its final home**, it becomes extremely vulnerable.

If another player lands on that same square:

Both pieces are immediately returned to the **starting area**.

This rule prevents safe endings and ensures that **no player can relax too early**.

Even players close to victory must remain cautious.

---

## 🎭 The Challenge System

The challenge system introduces the game's most unique psychological element.

Players may challenge any move they believe to be illegal or suspicious.

When a challenge occurs, the game determines whether the move was valid.

Two outcomes are possible.

If the move was illegal, the player who attempted it receives a penalty.

If the move was valid, the challenger is penalized instead.

This system encourages players to:

* observe carefully
* question suspicious actions
* take calculated risks

Sometimes a challenge can change the entire outcome of the match.

---

## 🟥 Cheat Tokens

Every player begins the game with a limited number of **cheat tokens**.

These tokens allow players to temporarily bend the rules.

Possible uses include:

* moving further than normally allowed
* escaping dangerous positions
* bypassing certain restrictions

However, cheat actions can still be challenged.

If the cheat is exposed, the player may suffer serious consequences.

Players must decide carefully **when deception is worth the risk**.

---

## ❄️ Freeze Mechanic

Capturing an opponent comes with a price.

Whenever a player captures another piece, one of their own pieces becomes **frozen temporarily**.

Frozen pieces cannot move during the next turn.

This prevents dominant players from gaining unstoppable momentum and keeps matches competitive.

---

## 💀 Missed Capture Penalty

Betrayal-Board encourages aggressive play.

If a player has a clear opportunity to capture an opponent but chooses to ignore it, the game may apply a penalty.

The piece that could have captured the opponent may be returned to the starting area.

This rule prevents passive gameplay and keeps the board dynamic.

---

## 🔥 End-Game Chaos

As players approach victory, tension rises dramatically.

The near-finish catastrophe rule ensures that leading players become prime targets.

Opponents may sacrifice their own pieces just to stop a player from winning.

Matches often shift dramatically during the final turns.

No lead is ever truly safe.
